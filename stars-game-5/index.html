I now have a half-complete monkey being rendered. (commit f1f6b15)

Note the very problematic right side!

I think this is happening because either a vertex is incorrectly inserted or a vertex is lost somewhere along the line.

I’m parsing what’s called a .obj file — an ASCII text format for representing 3D objects. Here’s an obj representation of a cube:

mtllib cube.mtl
o Cube
v 1.000000 -1.000000 -1.000000
v 1.000000 -1.000000 1.000000
v -1.000000 -1.000000 1.000000
v -1.000000 -1.000000 -1.000000
v 1.000000 1.000000 -0.999999
v 0.999999 1.000000 1.000001
v -1.000000 1.000000 1.000000
v -1.000000 1.000000 -1.000000
vn 0.000000 -1.000000 0.000000
vn 0.000000 1.000000 0.000000
vn 1.000000 0.000000 0.000000
vn -0.000000 -0.000000 1.000000
vn -1.000000 -0.000000 -0.000000
vn 0.000000 0.000000 -1.000000
usemtl Material
s off
f 1//1 2//1 3//1 4//1
f 5//2 8//2 7//2 6//2
f 1//3 5//3 6//3 2//3
f 2//4 6//4 7//4 3//4
f 3//5 7//5 8//5 4//5
f 5//6 1//6 4//6 8//6

It’s really simple — v loads a vertex, vn loads a vertex normal, and f loads a face (i.e. triangle).

The color of the monkey currently signifies the normals that have be loaded. You can kind of see how the color changes almost continuously depending on the orientation of the vertex. (Excluding the right side of course.) These normals will be used for lighting and shading the monkey, which is what I plan to implement soon.
